### Discovery 001: Flat-Chaos as Pipeline
**Category**: Architecture  
**Date**: 2026-03-19

**Observation**: The BaBbLE pipeline pattern (Source → Filter → Transmute → Sink) is identical to functional programming's map/filter/reduce, but with creative naming.

**Insight**: By renaming technical concepts with poetic language, the same patterns become more intuitive and creative. "Transmute" feels more powerful than "reduce".

**Application**: All new features should follow this pipeline pattern for consistency.

### Discovery 002: Entropy as Creative Fuel
**Category**: Philosophy  
**Date**: 2026-03-19

**Observation**: Adding controlled randomness (entropy) to animations creates organic, lifelike movement without complex physics simulations.

**Insight**: Perfectly mathematical animations feel robotic. A little chaos (Math.random(), sin waves with varying phases) creates the illusion of life.

**Application**: All procedural systems should have an entropy parameter to inject controlled chaos.

### Discovery 003: Streams as Universal Abstraction
**Category**: Architecture  
**Date**: 2026-03-19

**Observation**: Streams work for particles, animations, commands, and even user input. The same pattern applies everywhere.

**Insight**: A stream is just a sequence of things that can be transformed. This abstraction is universal.

**Application**: When building new features, ask "Can this be modeled as a stream?" The answer is usually yes.

### Discovery 004: Emergent Behavior from Simple Rules
**Category**: Architecture  
**Date**: 2026-03-19

**Observation**: Complex flocking behavior emerges from just two rules: low entropy attracts, high entropy repels. No complex physics needed.

**Insight**: The most interesting behaviors come from the simplest rules. By making the rule depend on an intrinsic property (entropy), we get organic, emergent movement without programming specific behaviors.

**Application**: Look for opportunities to create complex behavior from simple, entropy-dependent rules.

### Discovery 005: Spatial Composition as Mixing
**Category**: Architecture  
**Date**: 2026-03-19

**Observation**: Weaving (parallel) and mixing (weighted) are two different ways to combine streams. Weaving interleaves; mixing blends.

**Insight**: Spatial composition is just weighted averaging. By blending entity properties (position, entropy) with configurable weights, we create visual effects similar to image compositing or audio mixing.

**Application**: When combining streams, ask: "Do I want parallel (weave) or blended (mix)?" Both have their place.

### Discovery 008: Layered Composition Creates Depth

### Discovery 013: Dream Layers Enable Compositional Depth
**Category**: Architecture  
**Date**: 2026-03-20

**Observation**: Implementing dream layers with depth ordering revealed that z-ordering creates visual hierarchy without complex rendering. Background, midground, and foreground layers create depth through simple opacity and position adjustments.

**Insight**: Depth doesn't require complex 3D rendering. Simple layering with opacity and z-position creates the illusion of depth. The brain interprets layered transparency as distance, creating rich visual experiences from simple techniques.

**Application**: When visual depth is needed, use layering rather than complex 3D. Create layers at different depths, adjust opacity by depth, and let the brain interpret the result. Simple techniques can create profound visual complexity.
